# User Journey Maps — Limitless Travel Platform

**Product:** Limitless ("No boundaries, just destinations")
**Date:** March 2026
**Prepared by:** UX Research Team

---

## Overview

Three core journeys mapped, covering the full user lifecycle:

| Journey | Primary Persona | Secondary Personas |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Discover & Inspire | Maya (Solo Adventurer) | Sophie (Luxury Tripper) |
| 2. Plan a Trip | David (Family Planner) | Maya, Priya |
| 3. Nomad Base Search | Priya (Digital Nomad) | Maya |

---

## Journey 1 — Discover & Inspire

**User:** Maya, 28 — Solo Adventure Seeker
**Entry point:** Opens Limitless with no fixed destination in mind
**End goal:** Has saved a destination and a rough set of ideas to explore further

---

### Stage 1: Arrive & Browse
**Touchpoints:** Home screen, editorial cards, mood filters

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Opens app → sees editorial hero ("Go somewhere cold and dramatic") → taps a mood filter ("Wilderness & Solitude") |
| **Thoughts** | "Oh, this is different. It's not just a search box." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Curious, pleasantly surprised |
| **Pain points** | None yet — first impression is good |
| **Opportunities** | Strong editorial hook is critical here. First 3 seconds must feel inspiring, not transactional. |

---

### Stage 2: Explore Destinations
**Touchpoints:** Destination cards, map view, editorial detail pages

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Swipes through destination cards → taps Faroe Islands → reads editorial write-up with photos, best time to visit, "vibes", estimated budget |
| **Thoughts** | "I've never thought of going here. This sounds incredible." |
| **Emotions** | 😍 Excited, imaginative |
| **Pain points** | Worried about cost and how hard it is to get there |
| **Opportunities** | Surface practical info *within* the editorial (flights from UK: ~£180, best months: May-Aug) without breaking the tone |

---

### Stage 3: Save & Compare
**Touchpoints:** Wishlist / "Dream Trips" collection, compare view

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Saves Faroe Islands → also saves Svalbard → taps "Compare" → sees side-by-side card with cost, travel time, best months |
| **Thoughts** | "This is so much easier than opening 5 tabs." |
| **Emotions** | 😌 In control, engaged |
| **Pain points** | Wants to know more about what to *do* there before committing |
| **Opportunities** | Comparison view with "vibe match" score based on her saved preferences |

---

### Stage 4: Share & Validate
**Touchpoints:** Community trip gallery, "trips like this" section

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Taps "See trips people have done here" → reads a 5-day itinerary from another solo female traveller → feels reassured |
| **Thoughts** | "If she did it solo, I can too." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Reassured, motivated |
| **Pain points** | Hard to tell if community content is recent |
| **Opportunities** | Date-stamp community trips prominently; add "Last visited: 3 months ago" badges |

---

### Stage 5: Commit to a Destination
**Touchpoints:** CTA ("Start Planning"), transition to Plan flow

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Taps "Plan a trip to Faroe Islands" → taken to trip planner with destination pre-loaded |
| **Thoughts** | "Yes. Let's do this." |
| **Emotions** | 🎉 Excited, decisive |
| **Pain points** | None |
| **Opportunities** | Smooth handoff from inspiration to planning — no new login, no fresh search |

---

**Journey 1 Emotion Arc:** Curious → Excited → In control → Reassured → Decisive

**Key Design Insights:**
- Editorial quality is the differentiator at stage 1–2
- Practical info must be woven into inspiration, not separated
- Social proof from real travellers is a crucial trust signal
- Transition from discover → plan must be seamless (no friction wall)

## Journey 2 — Plan a Trip

**User:** David, 42 — Family Vacation Planner
**Entry point:** Has a destination in mind (Mallorca), needs to plan the full trip
**End goal:** Full itinerary with accommodation, key activities, and a shared trip link for his partner

---

### Stage 1: Set Trip Parameters
**Touchpoints:** Trip setup screen

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Taps "Plan a Trip" → enters: destination (Mallorca), dates (24 Jul – 3 Aug), party (2 adults, 2 children aged 8 & 11), budget (£3,500 total) |
| **Thoughts** | "Finally — something that actually asks about the kids upfront." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Hopeful, cautious |
| **Pain points** | Used to platforms ignoring family context until deep in the booking flow |
| **Opportunities** | "Family mode" auto-applied once kids are added — filters, recommendations, and UI copy all shift |

---

### Stage 2: Find Accommodation
**Touchpoints:** Accommodation recommendations, family filters

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Browses accommodation sorted by "family score" → applies "pool", "self-catering" filters → reads reviews tagged "families with school-age kids" |
| **Thoughts** | "These reviews are actually relevant to me for once." |
| **Emotions** | 😌 Relieved, engaged |
| **Pain points** | Still second-guessing whether the "family score" is trustworthy |
| **Opportunities** | Show the methodology behind family score; include "Stayed with kids aged X" reviewer context |

---

### Stage 3: Build the Itinerary
**Touchpoints:** Itinerary map, drag-and-drop activity blocks

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Limitless auto-suggests a day-by-day structure → David drags activities into place → adds "Palma Old Town walk" on day 2 → moves beach day to day 4 because of weather forecast |
| **Thoughts** | "I can actually see the whole trip in one view. This is what I needed." |
| **Emotions** | 😄 Satisfied, in control |
| **Pain points** | Unsure about realistic travel times between activities |
| **Opportunities** | Inline travel time estimates between activities on the itinerary map |

---

### Stage 4: Add Practical Details
**Touchpoints:** Trip checklist, document storage, reminders

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Limitless auto-generates a family checklist (passport expiry check, travel insurance, car seat, GHIC cards) → David ticks off items over the following week |
| **Thoughts** | "Oh wow, I always forget about the GHIC cards." |
| **Emotions** | 😅 Grateful, slightly embarrassed he forgot |
| **Pain points** | Some checklist items don't apply (e.g., visa for EU family); irrelevant items feel cluttered |
| **Opportunities** | Smart checklist that adapts to nationality, destination, and party composition |

---

### Stage 5: Share with Partner
**Touchpoints:** Trip sharing, collaborative editing

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Shares trip link with partner Sarah → she opens on her phone, adds a dinner reservation she found, comments "Can we do the boat trip instead of day 4?" |
| **Thoughts** | "First time we've both been in the same place when planning a holiday." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Collaborative, less stressed |
| **Pain points** | Notifications from Sarah's edits interrupt him during work |
| **Opportunities** | "Planning mode" vs "review mode" — notify on-demand, not real-time |

---

**Journey 2 Emotion Arc:** Hopeful → Relieved → Satisfied → Grateful → Collaborative

**Key Design Insights:**
- Context (family, party size, kids' ages) must be set once and persist everywhere
- Family-specific UX is a strong differentiator — no competitor does this well
- Collaborative planning is a top family need; solve the notification/distraction issue
- Checklists are loved but must be smart and personalised

## Journey 3 — Nomad Base Search

**User:** Priya, 34 — Digital Nomad
**Entry point:** Current Lisbon base ending in 6 weeks; needs to find next location
**End goal:** New base city selected, accommodation provisionally booked, community connection made

---

### Stage 1: Open-ended Destination Discovery
**Touchpoints:** Nomad search mode, "Nomad Score" filters

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Switches to "Nomad" mode → searches "SE Asia, under €1,200/month all-in, decent digital nomad scene, good weather Oct–Dec" |
| **Thoughts** | "Finally — a platform that treats me like a resident, not a tourist." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Seen, understood |
| **Pain points** | Worried the cost estimates will be tourist-skewed, not real monthly costs |
| **Opportunities** | Source cost data from nomad communities (Nomad List, local expat groups) and display range: "€900–€1,100/month for a furnished 1-bed" |

---

### Stage 2: Evaluate Candidate Cities
**Touchpoints:** City comparison cards, nomad score breakdown

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Comparing Chiang Mai, Canggu, and Penang → views breakdown: wifi speed, co-working spaces, visa length, safety index, average rent, nomad community size |
| **Thoughts** | "Penang — I hadn't thought of that. But the community looks smaller." |
| **Emotions** | 🤔 Analytical, cautious |
| **Pain points** | Data freshness — some metrics might be outdated |
| **Opportunities** | "Last verified" timestamps on key metrics; community-sourced updates |

---

### Stage 3: Verify Visa & Entry
**Touchpoints:** Visa information panel, country-specific requirements

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Checks visa requirements for her nationality (Indian passport) → sees 30-day visa-on-arrival for Malaysia + option to extend, links to official government source |
| **Thoughts** | "This is so much better than the visa nightmare I usually go through." |
| **Emotions** | 😌 Reassured |
| **Pain points** | Visa rules change frequently — needs to trust the information is current |
| **Opportunities** | Always link to the official government source; show "last confirmed" date and community reports |

---

### Stage 4: Find Accommodation
**Touchpoints:** Long-stay accommodation search (30+ day filter)

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Filters for "30+ day stays", "fast wifi confirmed", "co-working space in building or nearby" → books a furnished studio in Penang via Limitless partner |
| **Thoughts** | "Monthly pricing that actually makes sense. Not just 30x the nightly rate." |
| **Emotions** | 😄 Satisfied |
| **Pain points** | Doesn't want to be locked into a booking before she's sure she likes the area |
| **Opportunities** | "First 7 days refundable" tag on nomad-friendly properties |

---

### Stage 5: Connect with Community
**Touchpoints:** "Nomads in Penang" community, events, meet-up finder

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **User actions** | Joins "Nomads in Penang" group → sees a Thursday evening meet-up at a co-working space → RSVPs |
| **Thoughts** | "I might actually know someone before I even land." |
| **Emotions** | 😊 Excited, less anxious about loneliness |
| **Pain points** | Worried the group might be inactive |
| **Opportunities** | Show "X people joining next month" and activity level badge on community groups |

---

**Journey 3 Emotion Arc:** Understood → Analytical → Reassured → Satisfied → Excited

**Key Design Insights:**
- Nomads need a fundamentally different UI mode — this is not holiday planning
- Cost data must be real and community-sourced, not tourist-skewed
- Visa information is a trust-critical feature — link always to official sources
- Community and social connection is a core product feature, not a nice-to-have
- Long-stay accommodation with monthly pricing is a genuine gap in the market
