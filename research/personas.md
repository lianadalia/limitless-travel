# User Personas — Limitless Travel Platform

**Product:** Limitless ("No boundaries, just destinations")
**Date:** March 2026
**Prepared by:** UX Research Team

---

## Persona 1 — The Solo Adventure Seeker

**Name:** Maya Chen, 28
**Occupation:** Graphic designer (in-house, 9–5)
**Location:** Manchester, UK
**Travel frequency:** 4–6 trips/year (2 longer, rest weekends)
**Typical trips:** Solo city breaks, hiking trips, budget backpacking across a region

### Goals & Motivations
- Escape routine and collect novel experiences
- Connect with local culture, not tourist traps
- Flexibility — plans loosely, pivots constantly
- Instagram-worthy moments (but authentically)

### Pain Points
- Existing platforms push the same over-touristed spots
- Booking.com and Expedia feel like shopping for a flight, not an adventure
- Hard to discover genuinely off-beat destinations without deep Reddit dives
- Trip planning is scattered across 5 apps (Maps, Notes, Google Flights, Hostelworld, Instagram saves)

### Tech Savviness: 5/5
**Primary devices:** iPhone (primary), laptop (for bigger research sessions)

### Quote
> "I want to feel like I discovered a place, not like I followed a checklist everyone else already checked off."

### Key Scenarios
1. **Spontaneous weekend:** Opens Limitless on a Tuesday evening, types "somewhere cold and dramatic in 3 weeks, budget £400", gets curated destination cards with mood, not just price.
2. **Itinerary building:** Saves 12 places during the week via the Limitless browser extension, opens the app Saturday morning and drags them into a 5-day itinerary on the map.
3. **Sharing:** Publishes her Iceland itinerary as a public trip that other Limitless users can clone and adapt.

### Success Metrics
- Discovered a destination she hadn't heard of before using Limitless
- Built a full itinerary without leaving the app
- Saved 30+ min compared to her usual planning process

## Persona 2 — The Family Vacation Planner

**Name:** David Okafor, 42
**Occupation:** Secondary school teacher
**Location:** Bristol, UK
**Travel frequency:** 2 trips/year (1 big summer holiday, 1 half-term break)
**Typical trips:** Family package holidays (2 adults, 2 kids aged 8 & 11), beach resorts, theme park trips

### Goals & Motivations
- Reliability and safety — no surprises with kids in tow
- Kid-friendly activities + something for adults too
- Value for money (family travel is expensive)
- Minimal planning stress — clear, step-by-step process

### Pain Points
- Scrolling through hundreds of hotels that look the same, no family filter
- Hidden fees on Expedia/Booking destroy the budget calculation
- Hard to know if a destination is genuinely child-friendly
- Booking for 4 people across flights, accommodation, activities is fragmented and stressful
- Too many logins, emails, confirmations to manage

### Tech Savviness: 3/5
**Primary devices:** iPad and Android phone (shared family tablet often used)

### Quote
> "By the time I've compared three holidays I've spent a whole evening and I'm more stressed than when I started."

### Key Scenarios
1. **Browsing:** Opens Limitless with a "Family — 2 adults, 2 kids (8 & 11)" filter already set, gets destination recommendations with a "family score" and clear age-suitability notes.
2. **Checklist mode:** Uses the Limitless family checklist (passport renewals, travel insurance reminders, packing lists) that activates automatically when a trip is saved.
3. **Review trust:** Reads reviews filtered to "families with primary-school age children" — not just 5-star average scores.

### Success Metrics
- Booked a holiday for 4 in under 45 minutes
- No hidden surprises at check-in or during the trip
- Kids loved the destination — it matched the family-friendly description

## Persona 3 — The Digital Nomad

**Name:** Priya Sharma, 34
**Occupation:** UX consultant (fully remote, contract-based)
**Location:** "Wherever I am" — currently Lisbon, Portugal
**Travel frequency:** Near-continuous; moves to a new base every 4–8 weeks
**Typical trips:** Monthly base-hopping, 1 longer "retreat" trip to reconnect with friends/family per year

### Goals & Motivations
- Reliable internet + good co-working scene at each destination
- Cost-of-living awareness (rent, food, transport at destination)
- Building a sense of community in new places quickly
- Flexibility — visa requirements, cancellation policies

### Pain Points
- No platform surfaces the "nomad score" of a city (wifi, co-working, visa, safety)
- Booking.com and Airbnb are short-stay optimised — monthly pricing is buried or expensive
- Visa and entry requirement info is scattered and hard to verify
- Hard to find and vet co-living spaces vs. just apartments
- Loneliness — no social layer to meet other nomads or locals

### Tech Savviness: 5/5
**Primary devices:** MacBook Pro, iPhone, iPad mini (for reading/sketching on flights)

### Quote
> "I'm not on holiday. I'm living. I just happen to do it in a different timezone every month."

### Key Scenarios
1. **New base research:** Searches "SE Asia, good wifi, under €1,200/month all-in, October" — Limitless returns ranked cities with a breakdown: avg apartment cost, co-working scene rating, visa ease, digital nomad community size.
2. **Community:** Joins the Limitless "Nomads in Bali" group and sees a pinned weekly meet-up at a co-working space.
3. **Long-stay booking:** Books a month in a co-living space directly via Limitless at a monthly rate that actually makes sense.

### Success Metrics
- Found a new base city that matched all practical requirements in under 10 minutes
- Connected with at least one other person via the Limitless community at the destination
- Had zero visa surprises on arrival

## Persona 4 — The Luxury Weekend Tripper

**Name:** Sophie Laurent, 47
**Occupation:** Head of Marketing, financial services firm
**Location:** London, UK
**Travel frequency:** 8–10 short trips/year (mostly 2–4 nights), 1 longer holiday
**Typical trips:** European city breaks, luxury spa weekends, food-focused trips, cultural long weekends

### Goals & Motivations
- Quality over quantity — premium experiences, not budget compromises
- Time efficiency — she values her weekend as much as her salary
- Curated recommendations, not an algorithm of sponsored listings
- Surprise and delight — a new restaurant or hidden gem she wouldn't find herself

### Pain Points
- Can't trust that "Top 10" lists aren't paid placements
- Airbnb has too much noise; finding genuinely premium stays is hard
- Five-star hotel booking sites feel like corporate booking tools
- No platform understands that a "luxury" trip for her means intimacy and curation, not just price
- Hates long forms, account creation friction, and being upsold at every step

### Tech Savviness: 4/5
**Primary devices:** iPhone (primary), MacBook (weekend browsing)

### Quote
> "I spend all week making decisions. When I travel, I want someone to just tell me: this is the perfect thing for you. Done."

### Key Scenarios
1. **Editorial browsing:** Opens Limitless on a Thursday evening, browses "Editors' Picks — Autumn in Europe" — reads short editorial write-ups about each destination, not just star ratings and price.
2. **Weekend builder:** Taps "Plan a weekend in Seville" — Limitless auto-generates a curated 3-day itinerary with a boutique hotel, 2 restaurant bookings, and a private flamenco experience.
3. **One-tap repeat:** Has a "my travel style" profile that auto-applies her preferences (boutique over chain, walking distance to centre, no resort-style pools) to every search result.

### Success Metrics
- Discovered a restaurant/experience she genuinely hadn't seen elsewhere
- Full trip planned and booked in under 20 minutes
- Trip lived up to (or exceeded) the editorial description

## Synthesis — Cross-Persona Insights

### Common Themes

| Theme | Maya | David | Priya | Sophie |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery is broken | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Planning is fragmented | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Trust in reviews is low | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Wants personalisation | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Time is precious | ❌ | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Community / social layer | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Needs practical info | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |

### Design Implications for Limitless
1. **Inspiration first, search second.** All 4 personas want to be inspired before they search with parameters — lead with editorial content and mood-based discovery.
2. **Contextual filters.** "Family" mode, "Nomad" mode, "Weekend escape" mode — the platform should adapt its UI vocabulary to the user's context, not force everyone through the same generic search.
3. **End-to-end in one place.** All 4 personas described friction from switching between apps. Limitless must own the full journey.
4. **Trust signals over star ratings.** Curated, editorially reviewed content outperforms aggregated star ratings for all personas.
5. **Fast flow for time-poor users.** Sophie and David need to be able to plan quickly. Fast paths (e.g., "Plan a weekend for me") must coexist with deep-dive tools for Maya and Priya.
6. **Progressive disclosure.** Show the essentials first; reveal complexity only when the user wants it.
