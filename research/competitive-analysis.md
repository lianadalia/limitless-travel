**Product:** Limitless ("No boundaries, just destinations")
**Date:** March 2026
**Prepared by:** UX Research Team

## 2. Platform Profiles

### Airbnb
**Value Proposition:** Live like a local — unique stays and experiences anywhere.

**Target Audience:** Experience-seekers aged 25–45, both solo and groups.

**Key Features:**
- Highly visual home/experience search with map view
- "Wishlists" for saving and sharing trip ideas
- "Experiences" — local activities hosted by locals
- Flexible search ("I'm flexible", region/month pickers)
- Split-stays for multi-destination trips

**Strengths:**
- Aspirational, emotionally compelling UI
- Flexible dates UX is industry-leading
- Strong community trust via reviews + Superhost
- "Airbnb it" two-sided platform appeal

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- Not a full trip planner — no itinerary tool
- No flights or transport integration
- Hidden fees erode trust at checkout
- Not well-suited to budget travellers or traditional hotels

---

### Booking.com
**Value Proposition:** The widest choice of accommodation, fast and easy to book.

**Target Audience:** Broad — budget to mid-range, frequent travellers, business travellers.

**Key Features:**
- Enormous inventory (30M+ listings)
- Genius loyalty programme
- Free cancellation filter (strong UX differentiator)
- Map view with price overlay
- "Sustainable Travel" property badges

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive filters and search operators
- Very high trust (brand recognition + reviews)
- Deals and price comparison are core
- Mobile app is fast and functional

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- UI feels transactional and data-heavy; little inspiration
- No trip planning or itinerary building
- Review system can feel gamed / artificial scores
- Overwhelming choice without good curation

---

### Expedia
**Value Proposition:** One-stop shop — flights, hotels, cars, packages.

**Target Audience:** Family travellers, package holiday seekers, loyalty-programme users.

**Key Features:**
- Bundle deals (flight + hotel discounts)
- One Key loyalty points (cross-brand: Vrbo, Hotels.com)
- Price alerts and deal calendars
- "Things to Do" (activities marketplace)

**Strengths:**
- Bundle pricing provides clear savings
- Cross-brand loyalty is a strong retention tool
- Comprehensive travel insurance add-ons

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- UI is cluttered and feels dated vs. competitors
- Discovery and inspiration are weak
- Search results can feel pay-to-play
- Poor handling of complex multi-city itineraries

---

### TripAdvisor
**Value Proposition:** Traveller reviews and recommendations you can trust.

**Target Audience:** Research-heavy travellers, all demographics.

**Key Features:**
- The world's largest travel review database
- "Travellers' Choice" awards and curated lists
- Restaurant + hotel + attraction search
- "Experiences" booking (Viator integration)
- Forums and Q&A community

**Strengths:**
- Unmatched depth of user-generated content
- High SEO authority — users arrive via search intent
- "Bucket list" and collection features
- Review depth builds strong trust signals

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- UI looks dated; inconsistent design system
- Booking flow requires jumping to partner sites
- Fake review problem undermines trust
- No itinerary or trip planning tool

---

### Wanderlog
**Value Proposition:** The best trip planner — built by travellers, for travellers.

**Target Audience:** Avid planners, road-trippers, group travel coordinators, aged 22–40.

**Key Features:**
- Map-based itinerary builder (drag-and-drop)
- Collaborative trip planning (share & co-edit)
- Import from Google Maps, Instagram saves
- Route optimisation (road trips)
- Restaurant and hotel recommendations in-context
- Offline access (mobile)

**Strengths:**
- Excellent planning UX — fills a major gap
- Collaboration is genuinely useful for group trips
- Feels personal and empowering, not transactional
- Active community sharing public itineraries

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- Free tier is quite limited; paywalled features frustrate
- No integrated booking — must leave the app
- Discovery / inspiration is weak (planning-first, not dreaming-first)
- Limited content for off-the-beaten-path destinations

---

### Google Travel
**Value Proposition:** All your travel plans in one place, powered by Google.

**Target Audience:** Everyone — leverages existing Google account ecosystem.

**Key Features:**
- Price tracking and fare calendars for flights
- "Explore" destination picker (budget-based)
- Auto-imports trips from Gmail
- Hotel comparison with price history graph
- "Things to do" powered by Google Maps

**Strengths:**
- Deep data advantage (flights pricing, mapping, reviews)
- Zero friction — uses existing Google account
- Price history graph is uniquely trustworthy
- "Explore" destination discovery is genuinely useful

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**
- Feels utilitarian — no emotional resonance or brand identity
- No itinerary building beyond auto-imports
- Doesn't surface off-grid or unique experiences
- Purely functional; no community or social layer

## 4. Opportunities for Limitless

### Gaps to Fill
1. **Inspiration → Planning → Booking in one flow.** No competitor does all three well. Airbnb inspires but can't plan. Wanderlog plans but can't inspire or book. Limitless can own the full journey.
2. **Emotional brand identity with functional depth.** Google and Booking are functional but cold. Airbnb is emotional but incomplete. Limitless can combine both.
3. **Genuine collaborative trip planning.** Wanderlog has this but paywalls it. Making collaboration free and first-class is a differentiator.
4. **Curated, not algorithmic.** Every platform is overwhelmed by inventory. Limitless can win with curation — editorial quality, human-feeling recommendations.
5. **Off-the-beaten-path content.** All platforms over-index on popular destinations. Limitless can champion lesser-known gems aligned with the brand name.

### UX Patterns to Adopt
- Airbnb's flexible date search UX
- Wanderlog's map-based itinerary drag-and-drop
- Google's price calendar / fare tracking
- Airbnb's wishlist / save-to-collection behaviour
- TripAdvisor's community travel lists

### Patterns to Avoid
- Booking.com's overwhelming filter/results density
- Expedia's cluttered dashboard and pay-to-play listings feel
- TripAdvisor's outdated, inconsistent visual design
- Wanderlog's hard paywall on core collaboration features
- Fake/gamed review systems — prioritise quality signals over quantity
